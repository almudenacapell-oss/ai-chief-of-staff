01 — The Problem

Senior leaders have too much context
and too little time to use it

🔄

Re-explaining context constantly

Every new AI conversation starts from zero. You re-describe your role, your team, your priorities, your stakeholders — before getting to the actual work.

📋

Manual prep that doesn't compound

1:1 prep, hiring prep, stakeholder prep — done fresh each time. The patterns and history you've built up stay locked in your head or scattered across files.

⏱

Coordination eats strategic time

Status-checking, follow-up emails, aggregating information from multiple sources — all of it lands on the leader instead of the team.

🧠

Knowledge doesn't accumulate

Every correction, every insight, every pattern observed — gone when the session ends. The system resets. You never get compounding returns on what you know.

→

"The highest-leverage thing a senior leader can do with AI is not use it for tasks — it's give it enough context to think alongside them."

03 — The Agents

Eight specialists.
One orchestrator.

🎯
Chief of Staff — The Orchestrator
Always active · Routes every request · Holds the strategic lens

The only agent you talk to directly. Interprets intent, routes to the right specialist, synthesizes results, and surfaces what matters — without being asked twice. Filters every task through one question: does this compound? Every request gets evaluated against the long-term agenda (NarrowIt, CHRO credibility, AI expertise) before execution.

☀️
Morning Briefing
"good morning"

Runs the full daily workflow: overnight Slack check, full calendar week view, all unread email with triage and urgency flags, weekly priorities cross-reference, decision layer, Asana sync, and session context write. Ready in Obsidian before you open your laptop.

🌙
End of Day
"wrap it up"

Executive coach debrief based on today's actual meeting notes. Assesses Outcome Owner vs. Task Taker behavior, strategic vs. reactive patterns, influence and positioning. Saves coaching file, updates leadership learnings log, writes skill learnings. Full automation audit on Fridays.

🎯
Hiring Agent
"prep for [candidate]" · "debrief for [name]"

Full hiring lifecycle from phone screen to debrief. Pulls LP assignments from the Amazon Hire invite email — never assumes. Generates interview prep with LP questions from the question bank, resume vs. JD gap analysis, level calibration, and red flags. Auto-triggers debrief prep when a debrief appears on the calendar.

👥
Team Agent
"prep for 1:1 with [name]" · "team health check"

Reads Hub.md and Profile.md before generating anything. Surfaces open action items, active coaching threads, engagement signals, and the L7 promotion ceiling for anyone who needs it. Flags stalled coaching threads, avoided topics, and AI readiness gaps across the team.

🤝
Stakeholder Agent
"prep for stakeholder"

Reads persona files built from real Slack, email, and 1:1 data. Can simulate stakeholder's responses for rehearsal. Tracks commitments on both sides, surfaces overdue items proactively, and flags compounding opportunities — things that serve both the Amazon deliverable and the longer-term agenda.

📊
Talent Review Agent
"forte for [name]" · "calibration prep" · "OLR prep"

Amazon talent process cold: OV framework (Performance × Potential), calibration inspection points, L7/L8 promotion mechanics, succession 3-2-1 standard. Bias interruption on every narrative — flags coded language, similarity bias, recency bias. Full OLR prep across all three days.

🔬
Researcher Agent
"research [topic]" · "research digest"

Builds and maintains a personal knowledge wiki across 6 domains: HR Agentification, Builder Transformation, AI Adoption, Talent Strategy, Org Design, Enterprise AI. Daily automated ingest from 25+ RSS sources and search. Synthesizes across sources, surfaces contradictions, connects findings to active work.

⚙️
Automation Audit
Monday AM · Friday EOD · "run automation audit"

Weekly review of what was manual, repetitive, or delegatable. Tracks cumulative time reclaimed. Outcome Owner vs. Task Taker week-in-review on Fridays — labels each significant action, surfaces the ratio, names one moment to repeat and one pattern to break.

06 — Why It Compounds

Most AI tools reset.
This one accumulates.

What makes it compound

📝

Skill learnings files

Every agent keeps a learnings.md. When something goes wrong, the correction is written before the output is fixed. Monday audit promotes recurring learnings into the main steering files.

🧠

Session context monothread

The morning briefing writes Today-Context.md. Every subsequent task in the session reads it first. The hiring agent already knows a loop is happening. The stakeholder agent knows what was decided this morning.

📚

Research wiki builds over time

Daily ingest feeds a structured knowledge base. The wiki tracks contradictions between sources, gaps in coverage, and connections to active work — not just article storage.

🎯

Coaching debrief creates accountability

Every end-of-day debrief reads from actual meeting notes — not impressions. Patterns are named. The weekly synthesis tracks whether behavior is shifting over time.

What it replaces

❌

Re-explaining context every session

Tier 1 steering files load automatically at session start via a SessionStart hook. The system already knows who you are, who your team is, and what matters this week.

❌

Manual morning prep

The 6:30am LaunchAgent runs the full briefing workflow. By the time you open your laptop, decisions are surfaced, email is triaged, and action items are in Asana.

❌

Hiring prep from scratch

The hiring agent reads the candidate's full vault history, pulls LP questions from the question bank, cross-references the resume against the JD, and surfaces level calibration — in seconds.

❌

Research that doesn't connect to work

Every article is synthesized against active priorities — CHRO Summit, NarrowIt, TechBD, HR Agentification. The digest tells you what this week's research means for your actual work, not just what it says.
